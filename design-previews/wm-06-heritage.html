A new era of memorial — where artificial intelligence breathes life into precious memories, giving families a sacred space to grieve, remember, and connect.

Margaret Eleanor Collins, photographed at Cornwall, Summer 1978 — remembered by family as "the heart of every gathering."
When Eleanor's family gathered after her passing, they found themselves grasping at fragments — a laugh remembered differently, a story told in three versions. vspomin.ai was built for moments exactly like this. A place where every memory finds its home, where photographs live alongside stories, and where the AI avatar speaks with the warmth of someone who truly knew her. "We don't replace grief," says the platform's creator. "We give it somewhere to go."
